    2026 training participant 8

    From the perspective of someone who has been working in the field for four years, in a slightly different branch of IT than cybersecurity (SAP development, and previously e-commerce web development based on data from a different ERP system), I would say that 'it goes without saying that a single course isn’t enough to fully prepare someone for their first job.' At least that’s my general impression, and this applies to this course as well. However, the number of hours would have to be even greater and/or more difficult exams would likely be needed to motivate some people to actually spend a little more time studying between classes. On the other hand, as a cyber layperson, I consider the course content to be very comprehensive, and the instructors seemed extremely competent. I appreciate the more relaxed atmosphere, without the pressure to perform, constant competency checks at every stage, homework, etc. It would be nice to add even more practical examples; in my opinion, even a 15-minute “mini-lab” in every class would be useful. Though, of course, I remember that in a few classes the instructors booted up a VM or some prepared environment and demonstrated practical work, a great thing.

    What this course does well, in fact, superbly, is showcase the various “subcategories” of cybersecurity work. It lets you “dip your toes” into each topic to see if it’s for you or not, and it provides a solid foundation for you to develop your skills in the areas that interest you. That’s exactly what I was expecting when I signed up for the course, which is why I’m very satisfied.

    2026 training participant 9

    The course is of a high standard. In addition to more general presentations, the course places a strong emphasis on cybersecurity within organizations, which sets it apart from many others. This approach provides insight into the hierarchy and work of cybersecurity teams in companies, rather than just offering a dose of technical knowledge often taken out of context, which helps participants better identify the area that interests them.
